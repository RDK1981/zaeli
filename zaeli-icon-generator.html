What to do with the PNGs

  1. Click Download icon.png — saves to your Downloads folder.
  2. Click Download splash-icon.png — same, but 2048×2048 for the launch screen.
  3. Copy both into the app's assets/images/ folder, overwriting the Expo-default placeholders:
    Copy-Item "$env:USERPROFILE\Downloads\icon.png" "C:\Users\richa\zaeli\assets\images\icon.png" -Force
    Copy-Item "$env:USERPROFILE\Downloads\splash-icon.png" "C:\Users\richa\zaeli\assets\images\splash-icon.png" -Force
  4. app.json already points to ./assets/images/icon.png (line 7) and ./assets/images/splash-icon.png (line 45), so no config change needed. The Android adaptive icon PNGs stay as-is — iOS-first, Android is a later concern.
  5. Kick off a new EAS build to bake them into the app:
    eas build --platform ios --profile preview
    (Icon + splash are native assets, so a fresh build is required.)

iOS rules — the exported PNGs are opaque (no alpha), have no rounded corners (iOS applies its own superellipse mask on top), and use a flat #FAF8F5 background. All three boxes checked. Design tokens exactly match the app.
