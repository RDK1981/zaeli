Option 2 — Clean list rows

iOS Settings pattern
Pros

→ Extremely familiar — zero learning curve

→ Big tap targets — good for larger text users

→ Easy to add new items without breaking the design

→ Colour squares still give identity without overwhelming

Cons

→ Feels "generic app" not "Zaeli" — least visually distinctive

→ Takes more vertical space — more scrolling

→ Doesn't feel like a warm family app

Circular icons with labels below — iOS share sheet / home screen vibe. 4-column grid. Light, breathable, modern.

Option 4 — Bento mix

Zaeli-signature, premium feel
Pros

→ Visually rich — genuinely feels like Zaeli, not a generic menu

→ Hero "Go to Zaeli" gradient pulls Chat as the magnetic centre

→ Module tiles have captions — more informative

→ Mixed sizing creates hierarchy (modules > channels > links)

Cons

→ Most complex to build & maintain

→ More visual weight — less breathable

→ Hero tile uses lavender gradient, risks clash with chat identity

→ Longer scroll than Option 3

My honest take

Option 3 (Minimal circles) is where I'd land. It has the most premium, breathable feel — and the 4-column grid lets everything fit without as much scrolling as rows. The soft pastel circles match Zaeli's warm family-app identity without being busy. The "Share with Zaeli" top section (two horizontal tiles with labels) also gives the upload options the weight they deserve.

Option 4 (Bento mix) is a strong second if you want something more distinctive. The "Go to Zaeli" gradient hero is genuinely unique, and module captions add context. Trade-off: more complex, more visual weight.

Option 1 (Refined tiles) works but risks feeling like a duplicate of the Dashboard. Option 2 is safe but generic — wouldn't recommend unless we really want an iOS-Settings vibe.

Pick one and we'll refine further — I'd happily iterate on the winner (tighten spacing, tweak icons, finalise what goes into each section). We can also mix ideas across options if a specific element from one catches your eye.
