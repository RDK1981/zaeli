The four directions — pick one

  • Option A — Pure chat bubble
    Same soft-grey bubble as every other Zaeli message. Eyebrow + structured paragraphs + emoji per para + chips below. Brief reads as Zaeli speaking, not as a card. Calmest. Most chat-native.
  • Option B — Time-of-day tint
    Soft palette tint (peach morning, sky midday, lavender evening) + a tiny tag pill at top. No card border, no win banner. Palette ties brief to time without shouting. Best palette use without being heavy.
  • Option C — Letterhead style
    No bubble at all. Vertical gradient bar in time-of-day palette down the left. Looks editorial. Feels designed and intentional. Most distinctive but least "chat-native".
  • Option D — Sectioned with tags
    Soft-grey bubble with ✓ DONE / ↗ NEXT / ⚡ ONE THING tags inside. Most scannable. Best for users who want to skim. Most structured but adds visual noise.
My pick: B (time-of-day tint). Palette accent without a heavy card. Brief still reads as a chat message — just with a context-setting wash that signals "this is your daily check-in, not a regular reply". The tag pill at top (☀️ MORNING / 🌤 MIDDAY / 🌙 EVENING) gives Zaeli a clean signature without committing to chunky tags inside the body. Structure stays narrative-prose like Richard's preferred copy. Win banner = killed (collapse it into the prose itself when warranted).
